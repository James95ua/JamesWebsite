Overview of this website

Fit by science with James its your hub for clear, evidence-based advice on nutrition and supplements. The goal of this site is to cut through the noise, explain the science in plain language, and help you make smart choices that actually fit your life.

On the Supplements page, we look at popular products, what the research really says, and if they're worth your money - no hype, just honest information.

On the Nutrition page, you'll find practical guidance on building balanced meals, managing energy levels, and supporting your training and overall health, through food.

As you explore, you'll see images of real foods, meals, and common supplements to help you connect the information with everyday choices. Whether you're just starting your fitness journey or refining your routine, this site is here to give you tools you can trust

Who this website is for

This website is for people who are tired of extreme diets, magic pills, and confusing advice. Maybe you have already tried different approaches, lost some weight, gained it back, felt good for a while and then ended up in the same place. Here, we slow things down and focus on simple, sustainable changes that you can keep for years – not just for a few weeks.

It does not matter if you are a complete beginner, coming back after a long break, or already training regularly and just want to sharpen your nutrition and supplementation. The goal is to give you clear explanations and tools so that you understand why you are doing something, not just follow random rules from social media.

How to use this website

You do not have to read everything at once. You can start with the Homepage to get a general overview, then move to the Supplements page if you are curious about which products actually make sense, and finally explore the Nutrition page to improve your everyday eating habits. Think of it as a small library that you can revisit whenever you need a reminder or a new idea.

As you read, try to connect the information with your own life. Ask yourself simple questions: “How does my current breakfast make me feel?”, “Do I rely on sugar or energy drinks to get through the day?”, “Am I sleeping enough to recover from training?”. Small honest reflections like these can be the start of real change.

Our approach to science

When we say “evidence-based”, we mean that ideas on this website are inspired by real research and practical experience, not by trends or marketing. At the same time, science is always evolving, and human beings are not robots. That is why you will see balanced messages here: we talk about what the research suggests, but we also respect individual differences and personal preferences.

You will not find 100% guarantees or promises that “this one trick will fix everything”. Instead, you will see explanations of how the body works, what usually helps most people, and where supplements or nutrition strategies can realistically move the needle. The final decisions are always yours – this site is here to make those decisions easier and more informed.

What you will not find here

This website is not about shaming people for their weight, food choices, or fitness level. It is not about pushing extreme low-calorie diets, unsustainable workout plans, or expensive supplement stacks that promise “miracle results”. We are not chasing perfection; we are building a healthier, stronger, and more resilient version of you step by step.

Instead of fear-based messages like “never eat this again”, you will see practical guidance: how to reduce sugar without feeling constantly deprived, how to use hunger and fasting in a structured way instead of suffering, and how to choose supplements that actually match your goals. The focus is always on education, not on guilt.

Next steps on your journey

After reading this overview, a great next step is to visit the Supplements page and learn which products have the most evidence behind them, and which are probably not worth your money. Then, move to the Nutrition page to explore ideas such as improving meal structure, using intermittent fasting carefully, and reducing added sugar in a smart way.

Remember: you do not have to change everything in one day. Even one positive change – a slightly better breakfast, one less sugary drink, a small eating window in the evening, or a better understanding of supplements – is already progress. This website exists to support you in making those small steps, again and again.
